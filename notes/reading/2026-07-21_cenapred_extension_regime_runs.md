# 2026-07-21 — The CENAPRED extension, the regime break, and the Otis-leveraged null (read-log)

> Session: `OQ-HAZ-18` executed (`HAZ-CENAPRED-10`, `HAZ-STOCH-06`, `INT-19`) — the 2016–2024 extension inspected and accepted (publication-regime break documented); regime-aware refits show the +8.4 %/yr arrivals growth is regime/window-specific (ciclón bridge flat, $+1.4\,\%/\text{yr}$, $p=.17$); the pre-registered event-study gate re-applied verbatim on 2002–2024 ($n=100$ episodes incl. Otis) → **null reconfirmed** ($p=.734$); the 2016–2024 subsample "pass" collapses under leave-one-out (Otis leverage: CAR $+22.9$ bp at $[0,+1]$, fully reverted by $[0,+10]$).

1. **Pielke Jr. & Landsea — *Normalized hurricane damages in the United States: 1925–95*, Weather and Forecasting 13(3), esp. the normalization methodology (§2–3) and the trend discussion** (`[PielkeLandsea1998]`, §99): damage trends vanish once losses are normalized by wealth/population, not just prices. **Why:** `HAZ-STOCH-06`'s central inference move — the flat cross-regime ciclón bridge against the strong pre-2016 growth says part of that growth was reporting density/exposure, not climate; without this reading the two results look contradictory instead of complementary.
2. **Belsley, Kuh & Welsch — *Regression Diagnostics*, ch. 2 (leverage, influence, single-row diagnostics)** (`[BelsleyKuhWelsch1980]`, §99, new): hat values, DFBETA, and why one high-leverage observation can own an OLS slope. **Why:** `INT-19`'s verdict on the 2016–2024 subsample — HC1 $p=.019$ with pairs-bootstrap $p=.28$ is the leverage signature, and the jackknife (drop Otis → $\beta$ flips sign) is exactly the DFBETA logic; this is what stops a spurious "post-FONDEN the channel appeared" claim.
3. **MacKinlay — *Event Studies in Economics and Finance*, JEL 35(1), the cross-sectional-regression and inference sections** (`[MacKinlay1997]`, §99): CAR aggregation and the limits of cross-sectional inference with few, heterogeneous events. **Why:** the re-run keeps the `INT-18` design frozen (pre-registration), so every new conclusion rides on the *inference* layer — small-$n$ cross-sections, bootstrap vs analytic errors — that this survey frames.
4. **Cont & Tankov — *Financial Modelling with Jump Processes*, ch. 2–3 (Poisson and inhomogeneous Poisson processes)** (`[ContTankov2004]`): when a constant-intensity model is the honest choice and what an intensity trend actually asserts. **Why:** `HAZ-STOCH-06` drops the trajectory $\lambda(t)$ extrapolation ($\lambda(2025)\approx 69$/yr refuted by the observed 7–9/yr) and returns to homogeneous $\lambda$ with regime bounds — the modelling decision `OQ-HAZ-19`(a) must defend.
5. **CEPAL — *Manual for Disaster Evaluation* (DaLA)** (`[CEPAL2014]`): what "total damage at replacement cost" measures. **Why:** `HAZ-CENAPRED-10` treats registry rows and report tables as the *same damage concept* across the 2016 regime break — that comparability claim rests on CENAPRED holding its DaLA-adapted methodology fixed while changing the publication grain.

Open decisions these readings feed: the headline-$\lambda$ choice and the 2024-extenso swap (`OQ-HAZ-19`), the manuscript wording of the strengthened rate-channel null (`OQ-INT-01/02`), and the Mexican-$a$ recompute of $S_{\text{rate,eff}}$ (`OQ-MKT-12`).
