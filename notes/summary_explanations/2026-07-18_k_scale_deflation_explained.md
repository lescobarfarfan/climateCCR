# 2026-07-18 — The scale $K$, the pass-through $\beta$, and deflation, explained (`INT-17` / `HAZ-STOCH-05`)

> Summary-explanation note (`GEN-26`) for the session that closed `OQ-INT-07` (a)/(b)/(d): what the deflator does and why it runs *before* the damage threshold (in detail, as requested), what $\beta$, $K$ and $K_{\text{eff}}$ mean and where their numbers come from, how to read the real-terms refit and the first all-estimated EE/PE run, and every caveat carried forward.

## 1. What was decided

Four decisions, taken in this order because each one shapes the next: **(i)** losses are deflated to constant 2025 pesos *before* anything else (`HAZ-STOCH-05`); **(ii)** the loss→mark route is loss-to-price with an explicit pass-through, $\text{mark} = -\beta L / K = -L/K_{\text{eff}}$, with both $\beta$ and $K$ *estimated from data the repo already holds*, not assumed (`INT-17`); **(iii)** the price target is interpreted as the climate-exposed Mexican insurance book (CNSF hidrometeorológico + agrícola lines), so a mark is a per-event loss-ratio contribution in log-return form (`OQ-INT-04`, first half); **(iv)** the leading event set is `mayores_200mdp` — damage $\geq 200$ MDP in 2025 pesos — with `ciclon_tropical` as peril-pure robustness.

## 2. The deflator, in detail (`HAZ-STOCH-05`)

**What deflation is.** A loss recorded in year $y$ is in year-$y$ pesos; pesos shrink. With an annual price index $I_y$ (the INPC), the restatement to base-year pesos is $L^{\text{real}} = L^{\text{nom}} \cdot I_{2025} / I_y$. The wired index gives factors $\times 2.71$ for 2002, $\times 2.10$ for 2008, $\times 1.60$ for 2015: a 100-MDP loss in 2002 is the same *real* event as a 271-MDP loss in 2025. Average inflation over the estimation window 2002–2015 was $\approx 4.1\,\%$/yr.

**Where the index comes from.** `configs/inpc_anual.yaml` holds annual-average CPI values 2000–2025 from the World Bank indicator `FP.CPI.TOTL` for Mexico — a tokenless mirror of INEGI's INPC (via IMF IFS). A deflator only ever uses index *ratios*, so the mirror's 2010 = 100 re-base is irrelevant: restatements are identical to those from the INPC in its official 2Q-jul-2018 = 100 base up to rounding. The values were cross-checked against published INEGI annual-average inflation (2017: $6.04\,\%$, 2020: $3.40\,\%$, 2022: $7.90\,\%$ — all match). INEGI's own machine routes (BIE API) require a registered token; swapping in the INEGI series verbatim is the pending confirmation (`OQ-INT-07` b residual, `[INEGI_INPC]`).

**Where deflation happens in code, and why the ordering is load-bearing.** Restatement runs inside `load_climate_events`, *before* the `min_damage_mdp` filter — not inside `fit_severity` as first built in `INT-16`. The reason is the threshold: a bar fixed in *nominal* pesos is a bar whose *real* height falls with inflation. Concretely, "$\geq 100$ MDP nominal" meant $\geq 271$ MDP-2025 for a 2002 event but only $\geq 160$ MDP-2025 for a 2015 event — the bar fell $\approx 41\,\%$ in real terms across the window. A falling bar mechanically lets more late-window events qualify, which *fabricates* arrival growth in the thresholded set even if the real event process were stationary. Deflating first makes the threshold mean the same real event size in every year.

**How big the artifact was.** The nominal-threshold set (`INT-16`) showed major-event arrivals growing $+9.6\,\%$/yr; the rarity-matched real-threshold set shows $+8.4\,\%$/yr ($p \approx 1.4 \times 10^{-7}$). So roughly $1.2$ pp of the nominal growth was the falling-bar artifact — and the remaining $+8.4\,\%$/yr is the substantive finding: **major-event arrival growth survives deflation almost fully**. The residual conflation (real exposure growth — more wealth in harm's way — vs climate signal) remains, and its standard treatment is loss *normalization* beyond deflation `[PielkeLandsea1998]`.

**The rarity-matched threshold.** 200 MDP-2025 was chosen because it reproduces the old set's rarity almost exactly (270 events vs 274; $\lambda = 19.3$ vs $19.6$/yr; $\approx$ p86 of positive real losses). Matching rarity, not the nominal number, is what makes the nominal-vs-real trend comparison a clean isolation of the bar artifact: same-size trigger set, only the bar's time-consistency changes. By Poisson thinning `[ContTankov2004]`, the real-threshold set is still an exceedance-thinned Poisson process — "jump whenever any climate event does $\geq 200$ MDP-2025 of damage" — and a real-pesos bar is the only one whose thinning probability $1 - F(u)$ is time-consistent under inflation.

**What deflation did to each fitted quantity.** Unthresholded arrivals are counts, so `todos` $\lambda = 175$/yr and its $-1.2\,\%$/yr trend are untouched; likewise `ciclon_tropical` arrivals ($+8.5\,\%$/yr — a built-in consistency check, since a peril filter has no bar to sink). Severity *levels* restate: the median major event is $905.5$ MDP-2025, the median cyclone $291.6$ MDP-2025. Severity *trends* lose their inflation component: unthresholded severity growth falls from $+28.3\,\%$/yr nominal to $+23.1\,\%$/yr real ($p \approx 10^{-31}$) — the removed $\approx 5$ pp is (compounded) average inflation, as predicted in the `INT-16` note. And $\sigma$ barely moves ($1.211$, $2.944$): deflation multiplies each loss by a *year-dependent* factor, which shifts log-losses by a year-dependent constant, so it can change $\sigma$ only through the (weak) covariance between event year and log-loss — second-order, and observed to be negligible.

**One sharp new reading.** Within the leading set, per-event severity is *flat* ($-2.0\,\%$/yr, $p = .34$); within cyclones it is flat-to-declining ($-8.7\,\%$/yr, $p = .18$). The climate drift in the leading set is therefore carried (almost) entirely by *arrivals* — more major events, not bigger ones — which the built channel already injects with no code change via the trajectory intensity $\lambda(t)$ (`INT-13`). This weakens the urgency of the step-aware severity extension (`OQ-INT-07` c) for the leading variant, though it stays open for the unthresholded sets.

## 3. $\beta$, $K$, $K_{\text{eff}}$ — what they mean and where the numbers come from (`INT-17`)

**The route.** A CENAPRED loss $L$ is economy-wide damage; a mark must be a relative value shock to a *specific* portfolio. The chosen translation is $\text{mark} = -\beta L / K$: $\beta$ says *how much of the national loss reaches the book*, $K$ says *how big the book is*. Both are estimated, which keeps the thesis's "test the relationship" leg (`INT-09`) partly empirical even on the assumed-pass-through route; the full market-reaction (event-study) calibration stays the later robustness check.

**$\beta = 0.174$ — the pass-through.** Estimated as total insured payouts of the climate-exposed CNSF book (hydro `MONTO PAGADO` + agro, corrected copies per `DC-HAZ-CNSF-6`) over total CENAPRED discrete real damage, on the overlap window 2008–2015 (CNSF consolidados start 2008; the CENAPRED event base ends 2015): $84{,}627 / 486{,}200$ MDP-2025. Interpretation: about a sixth of national climate damage flows through this insurance book as claims. The yearly ratio ranges $0.05$–$0.78$ — which events hit insured stock varies enormously — so $\beta$ is an *average* transfer coefficient; applying it per event assumes proportional penetration across event sizes (documented approximation). The denominator uses the discrete-event universe deliberately: it is the same universe the jumps draw from, and the excluded annual-aggregate rows carry only $1.1\,\%$ of the window's real damage (the 2011 drought is recorded as discrete sub-year episodes, so drought is *not* lost).

**$K = 22{,}964$ MDP-2025 — the book size.** The book's annual real premium volume in 2024 (hydro $21{,}283$ + agro $1{,}680$), the actuarial exposure-base convention `[Klugman]`. Under it, $\beta L / K$ is the event's contribution to the book's annual loss ratio, and the mark is that contribution as a log-return on the book's value. The 5-year mean premium ($20{,}995$) moves $K$ by $< 9\,\%$ — the choice of anchor year is not load-bearing. Note the composition shift: the agro book collapsed after 2020 ($\approx 5{,}000$ MDP/yr real in 2008–2015 to $1{,}680$ in 2024), so today's exposed book is essentially the hydro lines.

**$K_{\text{eff}} = K / \beta = 131{,}932$ MDP-2025 — the single number the engine takes.** `to_mark_sampler(K_eff)` divides the fitted lognormal by one constant, which shifts only its log-median — **$\sigma = 1.211$ transfers exactly** (this is the one-constant case, unlike deflation's year-varying factors). Resulting marks: median $-0.69\,\%$, mean $-1.43\,\%$ (lognormal mean $= \text{median} \cdot e^{\sigma^2/2} \approx 2.08 \times$ median). The triangle closes: $\beta \lambda E[L] \approx 6{,}330$ MDP/yr of expected major-event payouts $\approx 60\,\%$ of the book's observed average annual claims, i.e. $\approx 28\,\%$ of annual premium — major climate events alone are worth about 28 loss-ratio points per year to this book.

**The currency call behind the CNSF sums (`HAZ-CLEAN-CNSF-14`).** CNSF SESA amounts are read as MXN regardless of the `MONEDA` label (which denominates the *policy*, not the reported figure). Evidence: reading `Extranjera` rows as USD units would put 2014 hydro payouts at $\approx 63$ bn MXN — several times any published figure for Odile, the year's dominant event — while the MXN reading gives a consistent $6.3$ bn in the occurrence year; and the `HAZ-CLEAN-CNSF-13` ×FIX error was precisely a *spurious extra* currency conversion in a delivery that normally reports MXN. Without this call, $64\,\%$ of hydro claim amounts would have been excluded and $K$, $\beta$ would be off by roughly $2\times$. External/documentary confirmation is queued in `OQ-HAZ-03`, with `[AMIS2014Odile]` (§99) as the figure to verify against.

## 4. The first all-estimated run, and how to read it

`configs/climate_jump_real.yaml` runs the EE/PE harness with every climate parameter estimated: $\lambda = 19.29$/yr, lognormal marks (median $0.00686$, $\sigma = 1.211$), price channel only. On the inherited PIMPA fixture book the shifts are small and land exactly where the equities are (CP 25 mean EE $+0.06$, CP 26 PE99 $-0.96$): the fixture book is rate-dominated, and the rate channel is deliberately off because a loss in MDP has no estimated basis-point equivalent yet. Two consequences: the big `INT-14` demo shifts came mostly from *placeholder rate marks*, and **the loss→rate translation is now the binding gap** between the assembled machine and a headline result (`OQ-INT-09`; natural route: an event study of CETES/TIIE moves around major CENAPRED events). The other gap is the asset universe itself: the interpretation of the targets is fixed (the insurance book), but the fixture's CS/UBS shares and EUR/GBP/USD curves are still stand-ins pending the Mexican calibration (`OQ-INT-04`).
