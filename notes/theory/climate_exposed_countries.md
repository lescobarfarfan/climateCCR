## 4. Key Research Insights

- **Climate Risk Significantly Impacts Sovereign Yields**: Studies from the **Asian Development Bank (ADB)** demonstrate that greater climate vulnerability has a **sizable positive effect** on sovereign bond yields, while climate resilience has an offsetting effect.
- **Climate Change Poses a "Triple Whammy"**: A **Center for Global Development** working paper finds that climate debt, high vulnerability, and low resilience all independently raise sovereign bond spreads in emerging markets.
- **Credit Ratings Are Responding**: ECB research confirms that higher temperature anomalies and more frequent natural disasters correlate with **lower sovereign credit ratings**, with acute physical risks (storms, floods) mattering more for emerging economies.
- **Eligible Bond Instruments**: The majority of these countries issue **USD-denominated Eurobonds** (tracked by the JPMorgan EMBI) and have **local currency government bond markets** (tracked by the GBI-EM). Data availability for USD bonds is generally superior and more standardized, making them a practical starting point for cross-country calibration of interest rate models.
- **Mexico as Benchmark**: Mexico is a major issuer in the JPMorgan EMBI and GBI-EM, with deep and liquid bond markets. This makes it an excellent benchmark for your research.
- **Caveat**: While some climate-vulnerable countries (e.g., Dominica, Honduras, Myanmar) top the climate risk rankings, they have **illiquid or non-existent sovereign bond markets** and are **not suitable** for interest rate model calibration requiring granular issuance-level data.
